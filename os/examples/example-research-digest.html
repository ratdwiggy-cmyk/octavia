02 · RESEARCH

The real problem was retrieval, not storage — most note-taking tools already solve storage

Show the reasoning that narrowed the problem to something solvable, not just a survey of tools.

[Describe what was investigated — e.g. "Compared a few existing approaches: a general note-taking app with tags, a bookmarking tool, and a plain folder of markdown files."]

[State the finding that reframed the problem — e.g. "All three could store the information; none made it meaningably faster to find later, because retrieval depended on remembering the right tag or filename rather than the content itself."]

This suggested the missing piece wasn't a place to put notes, but a way to search them by meaning rather than exact keyword — which pointed toward a lightweight search layer on top of a plain-text store rather than a new note-taking app.

03 · SOLUTION

[System name] — a searchable index over a plain folder of notes

Describe the shape of the solution before the implementation details.

[Describe the concept — e.g. "Notes stay as plain markdown files in a single folder — nothing proprietary — but a small index sits on top of them, allowing a search by topic or question rather than by exact filename or tag."]

Keeping the notes themselves in an open, plain format was a deliberate choice: the search layer can be replaced or dropped without losing the underlying notes — consistent with building for independence rather than lock-in.
